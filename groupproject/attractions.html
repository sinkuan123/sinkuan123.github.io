Attractions in Malaysia

Top Kedah Attractions

Chinatown

The Chinatown in Alor Setar is one of the best preserved Chinatowns in Malaysia dating back to the 1950s. The shophouses are mostly restored, each with its own living quarters, style, decor and colour. Here, the main business income is printing shop, bicycle repair, hardware.

Eagle Square

Eagle Square, also known as Dataran Lang, is a stunning 12-meter sculpture of an eagle standing on an elevated platform ready to take off. Kuah Jetty, Jetty Point Mall, Taman Lagenda Dalam Langkawi, as well as several restaurants and duty-free shops are all within a five-minute walk of this famous landmark.

Top Penang Attractions

Batu Ferringhi Beach

Batu Ferringhi Beach is an adventure playground where you can enjoy a relaxing holiday. The availability of jet skis and parasailing by the sea is the main lure that attracts every tourist who visits this small town of Penang. In addition to lying on the beach and sunbathing, you can also take a buggy ride, deep-sea fishing or take a banana boat ride on the golden sands.

Kek Lok Si temple

With a history of more than 100 years, Kek Lok Si Temple is Malaysia's largest and wonderful Buddhist temple off the coast of Penang. A haven for Buddhist pilgrims and Chinese teachings, the architectural structure has a seven-story pagoda and a large Buddha image in addition to thousands of Buddha images, more than a hundred carvings and some sculptures, making it a part of Mahayana and Theravada traditions home.

Top Melaka Attractions

A Famosa

A Famosa, a former Portuguese fort, is one of the oldest surviving remains of European architecture in Southeast Asia. At the time of its construction, A Famosa was the official residence of the entire Portuguese government. It consists of a long wall and four main towers. A Famosa is conveniently located along the North-South Expressway. For those looking to travel by rail, the site is just ten minutes away from Tampin Railway Station.

Top Selangor Attractions

Batu Caves

One of the grandest and oldest attractions in Selangor, the Batu Caves are a place of religious devotion and pilgrimage for Hindus. The 100-year-old temple of Lord Murugan, who defeated demons in Hindu mythology, is located in a cave on top of a limestone hill.

Sky Mirror

You can see a live miracle of nature at Kuala Selangor Beach, widely known as Sky Mirror. It spends most of its time submerged on the ocean floor, only emerging at ideal tides. That's why this place isn't even marked on the world map. The shallow waters of this secret island reflect the entire sky, offering unique photo opportunities.
