History of Malaysia

Malaysia's history began with the establishment of the Malacca Sultanate in the early 15th century. The country gained independence on 31 August 1957 as the Federation of Malaya. Malaysia was formed in 1963 with the accession of Sabah and Sarawak.

The birth of modern Malaysia took place in July of 1963. The Philippines claimed that Sabah was a part of its territory and broke off all relations. At the same time, Indonesia laid claim to Borneo and invaded parts of Sabah and Sarawak before giving up its claim in 1966.

Sri Maha Mariamman Temple is located in Chinatown, Kuala Lumpur. It is the oldest temple in Mariamman, the incarnation of the Snow Mountain Goddess, and the oldest Hindu temple in the city. The Mariamman Temple is beautifully crafted and designed with Dravidian architecture and has a Raja Gopuram tower at the entrance.

Located in Malacca City, the Malacca Sultanate Palace is a tribute to the Malacca Sultanate. It is located at the foot of St. Paul's Hill and gives you an insight into the ancient Malay kingdom that existed in Malacca. The Malacca Sultanate Palace is a replica of the 15th century Sultan Mansur Shah Palace. This palatial palace was built using traditional building methods without using a single nail.

St. George's Church is an Anglican church located on Farquhar Street in George Town. The 200-year-old shrine is the oldest Anglican church in Southeast Asia and one of the oldest buildings in Penang. The main highlight of St. George's Church is its recently inaugurated Heritage Centre, which provides an atmosphere of exchange among visitors. The original church was granted National Heritage status in 2007 and has been renovated several times.
