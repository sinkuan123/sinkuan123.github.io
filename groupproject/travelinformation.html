Travel Information

Travel Within Malaysia

There are various ways to travel within Malaysia. Most modes of transportation are also discussed on the many specific pages for destinations within Malaysia.

Rapid Rail

Rapid Bus

Rapid KL is a public transport system owned by Prasarana Malaysia and operated by its subsidiaries, Rapid Rail and Rapid Bus. Its coverage spans the Kuala Lumpur and Klang Valley areas, followed by the federal government's reorganisation of Kuala Lumpur's public transport system following the bankruptcy of STAR and PUTRA Light Rapid Transit operators.

Weather in Malaysia


Malaysia has a tropical climate. The average annual temperature in Malaysia is 25.4 °C. April, May, and June are the hottest months of the year. Rainfall is also high throughout the year, especially in November and December. Malaysia receives about six hours of direct sunlight each day and is most likely to be covered by clouds in the afternoon or evening.

Traveling by boat is not popular in Malaysia. There are no boat connections between Peninsular Malaysia and Borneo. Of course, there are also small boats that sail between the tropical islands and the mainland. Between the mainland and islands such as Langkawi and Pangkor, there are larger boats carrying many passengers to and from the islands.
