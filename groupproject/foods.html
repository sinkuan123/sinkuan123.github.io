Foods in Malaysia

Popular Food in Penang

Char Koay Kak

Char Koay Kak was delicious, slightly sweet, and had a nice fudge texture. At first, we weren't too crazy about this dish, but we quickly fell in love with it. This seemed to be the case with many of the Malaysian dishes we tried in Penang. It won't blow your mind right away, but you'll develop a taste for it by the end.

Popular Food in Melaka

Chicken Rice Ball

Hainanese chicken rice balls are basically fragrant chicken rice shaped into cute little golf balls. It is usually served with some tender and firm chicken or crispy roast pork. Remember to order the hot chilli sauce from the shop because the sauce can make the meal more appetizing.

Popular Food in Ipoh

Bean Sprouts Chicken

One of Ipoh’s famous signature dishes is boiled chicken with bean sprouts. A few seasonings and fresh side dishes, along with crispy and plump bean sprouts, rice, are enough to bring out the savoury flavour and smooth texture of the chicken. The best way to eat boiled chicken with bean sprouts is to dip some garlic chilli sauce on the boiled chicken to enhance the taste!

Popular Food in Johor

Tangkak Beef Noodles

Tangkak Beef Brisket Noodles with Kway Teow and Chunks of Fresh Pork, Tripe, and Beef Tendon, Garnished with Minced Ham Vegetables and Spring Onions. Check out this hearty cut of beef, Tangkak Beef, which makes me drool every time I look at it.

Popular Food in Klang

Bah Kut Teh

Going to Klang usually means having a taste of the famously filling bak kut teh. It has an aromatic flavour and a subtle sweetness. The herbs and pork that have been cooked for hours and infused into the broth. The meat itself is tender and juicy, and it goes well with the white rice.
