Penang 4 Days 3 Nights Trip

DAY 1
The first day, we will go to the Dhammikarama Burmese Temple. Located in Lorong Burma in George Town, this Buddhist temple was built in 1803 and features a serene white marble face and hands Buddha statue, a wishing pool, and some Burmese-style temple buildings, including the newly completed Golden Pagoda Clock Tower, which opened on January 5, 2011.
DAY 2
The next day, we will go to Batu Feringgi. This is the most popular beach area in Penang, and many resort hotels are located here. In addition to the normal beach activities, there are various dining and entertainment venues, as well as a night market where you can bargain for clothing, accessories, gifts, and souvenirs.
DAY 3
The third day we will go to Penang National Park. Here, you can follow rainforest trails to secluded beaches and coves such as Monkey Beach and Pantai Karacha Beach. There are sea turtle nesting grounds and possibly the only distributary lake in Asia. You can also visit the fishing village of Teluk Bahang near the park entrance.
DAY 4
The final day will be at the Logan Memorial. The Logan Memorial was erected in 1873 to commemorate Scottish lawyer, newspaper editor, author, and ethnologist James Richardson Logan, known for his defence of the rights of "little people" to be freed from official restrictions in colonial Penang.

Langkawi 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
Start your trip by visiting Pulau Payar Marine Park and indulge yourself in its vibrant-coloured coral reefs and graceful fishes. The marine park will surely be a great spot for you to get an amazing diving and snorkelling experience.
DAY 2
Explore the steepest sky cab ride in the world at Panorama SkyCab Langkawi for your second day in Langkawi. Get your lunch at the Sky Bistro and spend your evening by exploring nearby attractions such as the Sky Bridge, 6D Cinemation, 3D Art Museum, Sky Adventure Park, SkyDome, and SkyRex.
DAY 3
Start your last day in Langkawi by going on the Kilim River cruise for a sweet escape of nature, with the river exhibiting its majestic beauty of a mangrove forest. Alongside the serene sceneries of the forest, you will also be able to embrace nocturnal bats and feed various eagles that inhabit the area. Get your lunch at the Hole in the Wall for freshly cooked meals.

Sabah 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
Discover more than 4500 species of flora and fauna at Kinabalu Park, Malaysia’s first World Heritage Site certified by UNESCO and by trekking the verdant rainforest while appreciating the beauty of wildlife in the park. In the evening by browsing Kinabalu Park’s most sought-after attractions such as the Canopy Walkway, Butterfly Farm, Orchid Conservation Centre, and Rafflesia Garden.
DAY 2
Pay a visit to Kundasang's Desa Dairy Farm in the morning and backdrop of Mount Kinabalu and the view of the cattle grazing the area. Visit the Kundasang War Memorial, go paragliding and get a glimpse of the city's history.
DAY 3
Start your morning with a 300-metre hike to Bohey Dulang’s highest peak and embrace the endless scenery of blue sparkling seawater, the wide opening of the clear sky, and the lush green scenery of the island’s surroundings. Next, you can try other wholesome activities available in the islands such as snorkelling, diving, bird watching, and visiting the giant clam nursery.

Pulau Rawa 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
Enter and explore the underwater realm of Rawa Island by going on a snorkeling trip. Famous for its sprawling coral reefs, it is definitely a feast for the eyes as everything is crystal clear underneath the sea. Get up close and personal with its marine critters in the kaleidoscopic ocean.
DAY 2
Start your day by going on foot around the island. The Island Walk Trail is a fantastic way to get acquainted with Mother Nature. A good, brisk walk by the trail will introduce you to an unforgettable panoramic view of the great sea and also the neighbouring island near Rawa, namely Pulau Besar, Pulau Tengah, and Pulau Hujung.
DAY 3
Spend the last day of your trip to Rawa Island by doing nothing. Grab your sunnies and sunscreen, lay by the beach and treat yourself to the blissful, zen-like tranquility that Rawa Island has in store for its visitor. At Alang’s Rawa Resort weekend after-dark party. This is the chance to mingle and get to know other guests over a cocktail, a glass or two.

Pulau Simpadan 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
The popular dive site is the Barracuda Point. Staying true to its name, divers can anticipate being surrounded by a massive shoal of blacktail barracudas at one of the most treasured spots in the submerged peripherals of Sipadan Island. If you ever find yourself on the sandy shores of the island, this exhilarating experience is definitely something not to be missed.
DAY 2
Despite being famous for its marine splendour, Sipadan Island is also the island to go if you are the bird lover. The island was declared as a bird sanctuary by the government of North Borneo way back in 1933. Here, you can find about 47 known species of birds, such as the Sea Eagle, Kingfisher, and the Sunbird.
DAY 3
Wrap up your trip by answering the call of the sea, again. Instead of deep-water diving, you can snorkel for a more relaxed excursion. The clear, unpolluted sea grants you an unparalleled view of the flourishing hard corals. If you’re lucky, you might get acquainted with the star of the Sipadan shores, the bumphead parrotfish!

Pulau Kukup 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
In the famous Mangrove Rainforest Tour, you can experience the diverse flora and fauna in this wide uninhabited mangrove island. The unique biodiversity has attracted the attention of scientists and researchers worldwide. The park is home to several rare species of lizards, fishes, and monkeys, among others. You can hop on a boat to tour around the mangrove rainforest and see the long-tailed macaques.
DAY 2
You can climb on top of the six-storey tower that is Pulau Kukup’s Aery. This, you can enjoy the quiet and peaceful surroundings of the island uninterrupted, as well as taking a glance at other neighbouring islands within the region. The tower is also perfect for birdwatching so can bring along the binoculars.
DAY 3
On the last day of your trip, visit the quaint fishing village of Kukup Laut. The locals as they will take take you around and visit the village, you can also grab local products such as belacan, ikan bilis, and salted fish sold there as souvenirs.

Mount Kinabalu 4 Days 3 Nights Trip

DAY 1
Here, you can see the exotic flowers and plants thrive in the Botanical Garden in Kinabalu Park. And stroll on the well-made walking path while you feast your senses on the invigorating ambience of the blooming, vibrant flora of the garden. Such as, carnivorous pitcher plant, Showy Asian grapes and so on.
DAY 2
Poring Hot Spring is a hot spot for locals and foreigners alike as it is the perfect place to unwind during the weekend, just an hour’s drive away from the park.
DAY 3
For a more elevated view of the lush rainforest surrounding Mount Kinabalu, check out the Canopy Walk nearby the Poring Hot Springs. Constructed from planks of woods and ropes, the suspended walkway is not for the faint-hearted.
DAY 4
Bid farewell to Mount Kinabalu by visiting one of its many Rafflesia parks. The parasitic plant is known for holding the reputation of being the largest flower on earth. Rafflesia plants are relatively difficult to find elsewhere and records have shown that it can take up to one year to fully bloom.

Sekinchan 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
Sekinchan is a small and quaint district, nestled in the west coast of Selangor. You can visit and enjoy the Paddy Processing Factory and Gallery to gain more insights behind the humble origin of rice. You can also learn more about the traditional paddy processing methods, the history behind Sekinchan, discover different types of rice and so much more.
DAY 2
Next, you can go to the Bagan Fishing Village to get a glimpse of the simple life. Here, you can get to know how the local fishermen go about their daily routine as well as learning new skills from them, such as repairing the fishing net. You might even get the chance to listen to their stories out in the sea. At here, you can enjoy the inexpensive seafood delicacies such as crabs, prawns as well as a selection of saltwater fishes and so much more, in their own distinct style of cooking.
DAY 3
Make a pit stop at the Khind Galleria to gain insights into the electrical product manufacturing process. The gallery showcases fans and other electrical products made by the homegrown Malaysian brand as well as the history of the brand dating back from its inception in 1961.

Terrenganu 3 Days 2 Nights Trip

DAY 1
The Terengganu State Museum is the largest museum in Malaysia. At Taman Tamadun Islam, you will enjoy the picturesque Crystal Mosque and Monument Park and appreciate the scale monuments of various mosques around the world up close. The local delicacy is Keropok Lekor.
DAY 2
Here you can enjoy a plate of delicious Nasi Dagang. Visit the Pulau Duyung Fisherman's Village to get a feel for the local boat-building tradition and admire the fascinating handicrafts of boats built by talented craftsmen. In the evening, visit Teluk Ketapang Beach for Terengganu's notorious beach food; a delicious seafood feast served with crunchy treats. Continuing your gastronomic feast, visit Dapo Pata, the busiest little shop.
DAY 3
End your tour with a visit to the historic Pasar Payang. Souvenirs, handicrafts, apparels and unique traditional cakes are some of the best attractions to do in Pasar Payang. In Chinatown, stroll through the beautifully decorated alleyways displaying colorful art and murals, then end your day at Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque, Terengganu's floating mosque.
